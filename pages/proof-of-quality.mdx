# Proof of Intelligence

Centralized AI APIs ask you to **trust** the model behind the endpoint. The AI
Power Grid **proves** it. Every model on the network is continuously benchmarked
for real intelligence — you get **measured capability, not a precision label.**

## Why this exists

On a decentralized network, you can't take a worker's word for what it's
running. A node could claim it serves a full-precision model at long context
while actually running a badly quantized version at a fraction of the context.
And quantization is a spectrum: a clean quant can be indistinguishable from the
original, while a broken one produces fluent-sounding nonsense that fails the
moment real reasoning is required.

So the grid stops trusting the label and **measures the model itself.** A great
quantized model that passes is welcome; a "full-precision" model that fails is
not. We rank by delivered intelligence.

## How it works

**Validator nodes** continuously and unpredictably send **probe batteries** to
every model on the network and grade the answers automatically:

- **Structured generation** — render a chess position to SVG, emit JSON to a
  schema, write code that has to compile. Quantization damage shows up first in
  structured and spatial tasks, and a parser catches it instantly.
- **Reasoning** — math and logic with verifiable answers.
- **Long-context recall** — hide a fact deep in a long prompt and ask for it.
  This also **verifies the model's real context length**, not just the claimed
  one.
- **Instruction following & perplexity** — format adherence and a low-level
  signal of degradation.

The probes are **mixed into real traffic, procedurally generated, and randomly
timed**, so they can't be detected or pre-answered. There's no static benchmark
to game.

## What it produces

- A live **quality score and capability tier** for every model on the grid —
  effective context length, structured-output reliability, reasoning accuracy.
- **Smart routing**: your request goes to nodes that measurably perform;
  persistent under-performers are downranked and removed.
- **Collateralized quality**: operators stake AIPG to serve. Serve measured
  garbage and the stake is **slashed**. Quality isn't a promise — it's backed by
  skin in the game.

## What it means for you

- **Pick capability, not quants.** Choose a measured tier; the network guarantees
  the model behind it actually performs.
- **Trust that's verifiable.** No centralized provider publishes continuous,
  independent measurements of the model you're hitting. The grid does.
- **Honest hardware.** Declared GPUs are cross-checked against measured
  throughput, so capacity is real, not advertised.

> The last revolution was metered in kilowatt-hours. This one is metered in
> tokens — and on the grid, every token is proven.
